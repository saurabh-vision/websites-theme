## The Race to the Bottom

Many founders think the way to win is to be the cheapest. They price their Micro-SaaS at $9/mo, thinking it reduces friction. In reality, it signals 'unreliable' and 'amateur' to high-value customers.

### The Problem:
We audited a productivity tool that had 500 users but only $4,500 MRR. They were overworked and underpaid.

### The Audit:
1. **Support Burden**: Low-paying users often require the most support. 
2. **Perceived Value**: When a tool is $9, a business won't trust it for core operations.
3. **Churn**: Users who pay $9 churn for the slightest reason. 

### The Refactor:
- **Phase 1**: Removed the $9 tier. 
- **Phase 2**: Introduced a 'Pro' tier at $49/mo with 3 premium features.
- **Phase 3**: Added an 'Enterprise' starter at $199/mo (annual only).

**Result:** MRR doubled in 3 months despite losing 20% of the customer base. You want better customers, not more customers.
