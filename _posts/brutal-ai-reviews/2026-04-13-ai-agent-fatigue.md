## Enough with the 'Agents'

Every founder on X is building an 'Autonomous Agent' that can supposedly run their entire marketing, coding, and sales pipeline. Spoiler alert: Most of them are just wrapped API calls that fail at the first sign of ambiguity.

### The Real Problem:
1. **Decision Fatigue**: When you hand over 100% of a task to an agent, you lose the 'why' behind the results. 
2. **Quality Decay**: AI agents are great at 0 to 1. They are terrible at 0.9 to 1.0. That last 10% is where your brand authority actually lives.
3. **Product Bloat**: Adding an agent to your SaaS just because it's a trend is the 2026 version of putting a 'Made with AI' badge on a calculator.

### The Solution:
Use AI for **augmentation**, not **replacement**. The best founders use agents to clear technical debt, automate repetitive parsing, and draft initial structures—but they never hit 'publish' without a manual audit.

**My Rule:** If you can't explain exactly how the agent reached its conclusion, you shouldn't be using it for your business core logic.
